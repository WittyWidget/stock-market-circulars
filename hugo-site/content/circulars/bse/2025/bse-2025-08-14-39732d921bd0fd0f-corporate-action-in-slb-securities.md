## Summary

BSE has issued a comprehensive list of upcoming corporate actions for Securities Lending and Borrowing (SLB) securities. The circular details dividend payments, bonus issues, and rights issues for 32+ listed companies with their respective record dates, ex-dates, and shut periods scheduled between August 14, 2025, and September 23, 2025.

## Key Points

- 32+ companies have announced various corporate actions including dividends, bonus issues, and rights issues
- Record dates span from August 14, 2025, to September 23, 2025
- Most actions involve dividend payments with varying shut periods
- Karur Vysya Bank has announced a bonus issue with record date August 26, 2025
- LLOYDS Enterprises has announced a rights issue of equity shares with record date August 14, 2025
- All securities will have trading restrictions during their respective shut periods

## Regulatory Changes

No regulatory changes specified in this circular - this is a standard notification of corporate actions.

## Compliance Requirements

- Securities will be unavailable for lending/borrowing during specified shut periods
- Investors must hold securities before ex-dates to be eligible for corporate actions
- Market participants must adjust their SLB positions accordingly during shut periods
- Brokers and intermediaries must inform clients about upcoming corporate actions

## Important Dates

**Immediate Actions:**
- August 14, 2025: Ex-date for International Gemmological Institute (record date August 15)
- August 14, 2025: Record and ex-date for LLOYDS rights issue and Venky's dividend
- August 18, 2025: Record date for Aarti Industries and Natco Pharma dividends
- August 19, 2025: Record date for Refex Industries dividend

**Key Upcoming Dates:**
- August 22, 2025: Jindal Steel & Power and Jindal Stainless dividends
- August 26, 2025: Karur Vysya Bank bonus issue
- September 2025: Multiple dividend record dates throughout the month

## Impact Assessment

**Market Impact:** Medium - affects trading and lending/borrowing activities for 32+ securities during their respective shut periods. Investors holding these securities will benefit from announced corporate actions.

**Operational Impact:** Medium - market participants need to adjust SLB positions and inform clients about eligibility requirements and shut periods for smooth execution of corporate actions.
