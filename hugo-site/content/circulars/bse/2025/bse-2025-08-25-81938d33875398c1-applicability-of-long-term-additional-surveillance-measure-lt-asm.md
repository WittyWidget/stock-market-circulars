## Summary

BSE has updated the Long Term Additional Surveillance Measure (LT-ASM) framework effective August 26, 2025. Seven new securities have been shortlisted for inclusion in LT-ASM Stage I, while one security (Ladam Affordable Housing Ltd) will move to ASM Stage II. No securities are moving out of the framework or to lower stages.

## Key Points

- 7 securities newly shortlisted for Long Term ASM Stage I framework
- 1 security (Ladam Affordable Housing Ltd) moving from Stage I to Stage II
- No securities moving to lower stages or exiting the framework
- Consolidated list includes over 500 securities under various ASM stages
- Framework applies enhanced surveillance and trading restrictions

## Regulatory Changes

- Implementation of Long Term ASM framework for identified securities
- Securities under LT-ASM subject to additional surveillance measures
- Enhanced monitoring for price movements and trading patterns
- Potential trading restrictions based on ASM stage classification

## Compliance Requirements

- Market participants must comply with enhanced surveillance requirements
- Additional margin requirements may apply for securities under LT-ASM
- Brokers must ensure proper risk management for affected securities
- Investors should be aware of restricted trading conditions

## Important Dates

- **Effective Date**: August 26, 2025
- All changes to LT-ASM classification take effect from market opening on this date

## Impact Assessment

**Trading Impact**: Securities under LT-ASM face enhanced surveillance which may result in trading restrictions, higher margins, and reduced liquidity. **Market Impact**: The framework aims to protect investor interests by identifying and monitoring securities with unusual price movements or low liquidity. **Investor Impact**: Traders and investors in these securities should expect potential limitations on trading activities and increased compliance requirements.
