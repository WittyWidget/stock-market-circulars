## Summary

BSE has listed new commercial papers issued by Poonawalla Fincorp Limited on a private placement basis on the debt segment with effect from October 24, 2025. The issuance consists of 5,000 commercial papers with a face value of Rs. 5,00,000 each, rated CARE A1+ and CRISIL A1+. The instruments will be traded exclusively in dematerialized form.

## Key Points

- 5,000 commercial papers listed with face value of Rs. 5,00,000 each
- Scrip Code: 728648, Scrip ID: PFL230125
- ISIN Number: INE511C14XZ6 (further listing under same ISIN)
- Issue Price: Rs. 4,91,781.50 per unit
- Market lot size: 1 unit
- Credit Rating: CARE A1+ and CRISIL A1+
- Trading denomination: Rs. 5 lakhs and multiples thereof
- Tick size: 1 paise
- ICICI Bank Limited appointed as Issuing and Paying Agent
- Trading only in dematerialized form

## Regulatory Changes

No regulatory changes introduced. This is a routine listing announcement.

## Compliance Requirements

- Trading members must trade these securities only in dematerialized form under ISIN INE511C14XZ6
- Trading must be conducted in standard denomination of Rs. 5 lakhs and multiples thereof
- Members requiring clarification should contact the debt department at specified phone numbers: 22728352/8597/8995/5753/8915

## Important Dates

- **Allotment Date**: October 23, 2025
- **Listing Date**: October 24, 2025
- **Redemption Date**: January 23, 2026
- **Notice Date**: October 24, 2025

## Impact Assessment

**Market Impact**: Minimal. This is a standard commercial paper listing by Poonawalla Fincorp Limited on a private placement basis. The listing expands the debt market offerings but has limited direct impact on broader market operations.

**Investor Impact**: Low. The commercial paper is available for trading on BSE's debt segment with a 92-day tenure (October 23, 2025 to January 23, 2026). The high credit ratings (CARE A1+, CRISIL A1+) indicate strong creditworthiness.

**Operational Impact**: Routine operational procedures apply for debt segment trading members. Standard trading protocols for debt instruments continue to apply.
