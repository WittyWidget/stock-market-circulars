## Summary

Embassy Office Parks REIT has listed new commercial papers on BSE Debt segment effective October 17, 2025. The issuance comprises 8000 commercial papers issued on private placement basis, each with a face value of Rs. 5,00,000. The securities carry CARE A1+ and CRISIL A1+ credit ratings and will be traded only in dematerialized form. ICICI Bank Limited acts as the Issuing and Paying Agent.

## Key Points

- 8000 commercial papers of Rs. 5,00,000 each listed on BSE Debt segment
- Issue price: Rs. 479,444 per commercial paper
- Total issue size: Rs. 400 crores (8000 Ã— Rs. 5,00,000)

- Detail Name: EOPR-16-6-26-CP
- Market lot: 1 unit

- Standard denomination: Rs. 5 lakhs and multiples thereof
- Tick size: 1 paise
- Issuing and Paying Agent: ICICI Bank Limited

## Regulatory Changes

No regulatory changes introduced. This is a standard listing notification for privately placed commercial papers.

## Compliance Requirements

- Trading members must trade these securities only in dematerialized form under ISIN INE041014072
- Trading shall take place in standard denomination of Rs. 5 lakhs and multiples thereof
- Tick size of 1 paise must be maintained for these securities
- Trading members requiring clarification may contact BSE debt department on 22728352/8597/8995/5753/8915

## Important Dates

- **Allotment Date**: October 16, 2025
- **Listing Date**: October 17, 2025
- **Redemption Date**: June 16, 2026
- **Tenure**: Approximately 8 months

## Impact Assessment

**Market Impact**: Low - This is a routine commercial paper listing on private placement basis. The securities will be available for trading on the debt segment for institutional and qualified investors.

**Operational Impact**: Minimal - Trading members can now trade these commercial papers on the BSE Debt platform following standard debt trading procedures.

**Investor Impact**: Low - The commercial papers carry strong credit ratings (A1+ from both CARE and CRISIL), indicating highest degree of safety regarding timely payment of financial obligations. The short tenure (8 months) provides a near-term investment option for debt investors.
