## Summary

BSE has listed new debt securities issued by SRG Housing Finance Limited on private placement basis with effect from October 27, 2025. The securities comprise 2,600 units of non-convertible debentures with a face value of Rs. 1,00,000 each, totaling Rs. 26 crore. The debentures carry an interest rate of 11.52% per annum with monthly interest payments and mature on October 24, 2031.

## Key Points

- Quantity: 2,600 debentures with market lot size of 1
- Scrip Code: 977242, Scrip ID: 1152SRHF31
- ISIN: INE559N07066
- Face Value and Issue Price: Rs. 1,00,000 per debenture
- Credit Rating: Acuite BBB+/Positive
- Interest Rate: 11.52% per annum (monthly payment)
- Date of Allotment: October 24, 2025
- Trading only in dematerialized form
- Tick size: 1 paise
- No put/call options available

## Regulatory Changes

No regulatory changes introduced. This is a standard listing notification for privately placed debt securities.

## Compliance Requirements

- Trading members must trade these securities only in dematerialized form under ISIN INE559N07066
- Members should refer to the Placement Memorandum available at BSE's debt section for complete details
- For clarifications, members may contact the debt department at specified phone numbers: 22728352/8597/8995/5753/8915

## Important Dates

- **Date of Allotment**: October 24, 2025
- **Listing Date**: October 27, 2025
- **First Interest Payment**: November 24, 2025
- **Interest Payment Frequency**: Monthly (24th of each month)
- **Interest Payment Period**: November 24, 2025 to October 24, 2031
- **Maturity/Redemption Date**: October 24, 2031

## Impact Assessment

This listing has minimal market-wide impact as it pertains to privately placed debt securities with a limited investor base. The debentures are rated BBB+/Positive by Acuite, indicating adequate safety with positive outlook. The 11.52% interest rate reflects the credit profile and current market conditions for housing finance companies. This listing provides liquidity options for institutional investors who participated in the private placement and adds to the debt market depth in the housing finance sector.
