## Summary

BSE has issued a notice confirming that no further action will be taken against RNIT AI Solutions Ltd. (Scrip Code: 517286, formerly known as Autopal Industries Ltd.) as the company has complied with the outstanding Annual Listing Fees (ALF) payment requirement. This follows previous notices dated October 15, 2025, June 25, 2021, and March 03, 2020 regarding companies that failed to pay ALF as per Regulation 14 of SEBI (Listing Obligations and Disclosure Requirements) Regulations, 2015.

## Key Points

- RNIT AI Solutions Ltd. (Scrip Code: 517286) has successfully paid outstanding Annual Listing Fees
- No further enforcement action will be taken against the company
- Company was formerly known as Autopal Industries Ltd.
- Compliance achieved with Regulation 14 of SEBI LODR Regulations, 2015
- Trading members are notified of the compliance status

## Regulatory Changes

No new regulatory changes. This circular confirms compliance with existing Annual Listing Fees requirements under Regulation 14 of SEBI (Listing Obligations and Disclosure Requirements) Regulations, 2015.

## Compliance Requirements

The company has satisfied its obligation to pay Annual Listing Fees as mandated under SEBI LODR Regulations. No further compliance action is required from RNIT AI Solutions Ltd. at this time.

## Important Dates

- **October 23, 2025**: Notice date confirming compliance and no further action
- **October 15, 2025**: Previous continuation notice (Reference: 20251015-16)
- **June 25, 2021**: Original reference notice (20210625-43)
- **March 03, 2020**: Original reference notice (20200303-40)

## Impact Assessment

**Market Impact**: Minimal. This is a positive administrative update affecting a single company.

**Company Impact**: Positive for RNIT AI Solutions Ltd. as it removes the threat of further enforcement action and demonstrates regulatory compliance.

**Trading Impact**: No restrictions or changes to trading status. Normal trading continues.

**Investor Impact**: Neutral to slightly positive for RNIT shareholders as compliance reduces regulatory risk.
