## Summary

BSE has issued a schedule of corporate actions for Securities Lending and Borrowing (SLB) securities covering 14 companies. The schedule includes dividend payments, bonus issues, and scheme of arrangement with specific record dates, ex-dates, and shut periods during which SLB trading will be restricted.

## Key Points

- 14 companies are scheduled for various corporate actions between August 22, 2025 and September 19, 2025
- Most actions are dividend payments with one bonus issue (Godfrey Phillips India) and one scheme of arrangement (TVS Motor Company)
- SLB shut periods range from 2-4 days for each corporate action
- Ex-dates generally align with record dates, with some companies having ex-date one day prior

## Important Dates

**August 2025:**
- August 22: AGI Greenpac Limited (Dividend) - Shut period: Aug 21-22
- August 25: TVS Motor Company (Scheme of Arrangement) - Shut period: Aug 22-25
- August 28: Route Mobile Limited (Dividend) - Shut period: Aug 26-28

**September 2025:**
- September 2: Gabriel India Ltd. and EPL Limited (Dividend)
- September 3: Patanjali Foods Limited and Prestige Estates Projects Limited (Dividend)
- September 4-5: Gujarat Gas Limited and Shipping Corporation Of India Ltd (Dividend)
- September 12: RHI Magnesita India Limited (Dividend)
- September 15: Aarti Pharmalabs Limited and LUX Industries Limited (Dividend)
- September 16: Godfrey Phillips India Ltd. (Bonus Issue)
- September 19: Gujarat Alkalis & Chemicals Ltd. (Dividend)

## Compliance Requirements

- SLB participants must note the shut periods when lending and borrowing will not be permitted for specified securities
- Market participants should adjust their SLB positions before the commencement of shut periods
- All participants must comply with the specified record dates and ex-dates for corporate action entitlements

## Impact Assessment

**Trading Impact:**
- SLB trading will be suspended for specified securities during shut periods
- Market liquidity may be affected for these securities during restriction periods
- Participants need to plan SLB strategies around these corporate action dates

**Operational Impact:**
- Multiple securities affected across different sectors
- Staggered timeline reduces concentrated impact on single trading day
- Clear advance notice allows participants to adjust positions appropriately
