## Summary

BSE has issued a trading suspension notice for commercial papers (CPs) of nine companies scheduled for redemption. Trading members are advised not to deal in these securities from the specified no-dealings dates. The suspensions range from October 29 to October 31, 2025, corresponding to the record dates set by respective issuers.

## Key Points

- Nine companies have fixed record dates for commercial paper redemption
- Trading suspension applies to debt market segment only
- No dealings permitted from specified dates for each security
- Notice issued under Corporate Actions category (Notice No: 20251024-6)
- Issued by Assistant Vice President â€“ Listing Operations (CRD)

## Compliance Requirements

- Trading members must not execute any trades in the specified commercial papers from the no-dealings dates
- All trading activities must cease one day before the record date for each security
- Members must ensure compliance with circular references DR-745/2025-2026, DR-746/2025-2026, and DR-747/2025-2026

## Important Dates

- **October 24, 2025**: Notice date and effective date of circular
- **October 29, 2025**: Record date and no-dealings start date for 5 CPs (Axis Finance, Standard Chartered Capital, Bajaj Housing Finance, KEC International, Nuvama Wealth)
- **October 30, 2025**: Record date and no-dealings start date for SBICAP Securities CP; Redemption date for first 5 CPs
- **October 31, 2025**: Record date and no-dealings start date for 3 CPs (Network 18, Indian Oil, Tata Housing); Redemption date for SBICAP Securities CP
- **November 3, 2025**: Redemption date for final 3 CPs

## Impact Assessment

**Market Impact**: Limited to debt market participants holding or trading these specific commercial papers. The suspension is procedural and necessary for redemption processing.

**Investor Impact**: CP holders will receive redemption proceeds on maturity dates. No secondary market liquidity available during suspension period.

**Operational Impact**: Trading members must update their systems to block trades in these securities from specified dates. The short notice period (5 days for first set) requires prompt action.

**Scope**: Affects institutional investors and high-net-worth individuals typically participating in CP markets. The companies represent diverse sectors including financial services, infrastructure, media, and energy.
