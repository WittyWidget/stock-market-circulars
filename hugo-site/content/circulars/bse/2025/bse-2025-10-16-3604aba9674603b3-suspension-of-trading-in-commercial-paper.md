## Summary

BSE has issued Notice No. 20251016-29 suspending trading in commercial paper of 12 companies effective from October 23-24, 2025. The suspensions are due to scheduled redemptions with record dates falling between October 23-26, 2025. Trading members are advised not to deal in the specified commercial papers from the "no dealings" dates.

## Key Points

- 12 companies have fixed record dates for commercial paper redemption
- Trading suspension affects debt segment instruments
- First suspension starts October 23, 2025 (ECL Finance Limited)
- Majority of suspensions (11 out of 12) effective from October 24, 2025
- Record dates range from October 23-26, 2025
- Each company issued under different ISIN codes and reference numbers

## Regulatory Changes

No new regulatory changes introduced. This circular implements existing regulations requiring trading suspension in commercial paper ahead of redemption record dates.

## Compliance Requirements

- Trading members must not deal in the specified commercial papers from the "no dealings" dates
- Members should update their trading systems to block transactions in these instruments
- Ensure clients holding these commercial papers are informed about the redemption schedule
- Settlement of existing trades must be completed before suspension dates

## Important Dates

- **October 16, 2025**: Notice issued
- **October 23, 2025**: ECL Finance CP trading suspension begins; record date for ECL Finance
- **October 24, 2025**: Trading suspension begins for 11 companies; record date for 10 companies
- **October 26, 2025**: Record date for Navi Finserv Limited

## Impact Assessment

**Market Impact**: Medium - affects debt market liquidity for 12 commercial paper instruments temporarily. Investors holding these instruments will receive redemption proceeds as per schedule.

**Operational Impact**: Trading desks and settlement systems need to update instrument status. Brokers must inform clients about trading restrictions.

**Investor Impact**: Holders of these commercial papers will receive redemption payments. No trading opportunity during suspension period, but this is a standard pre-redemption process.

**Sector Focus**: Primarily impacts financial services sector (9 out of 12 companies) including brokerages, NBFCs, and financial services firms, plus one manufacturing company (Tata Steel).
