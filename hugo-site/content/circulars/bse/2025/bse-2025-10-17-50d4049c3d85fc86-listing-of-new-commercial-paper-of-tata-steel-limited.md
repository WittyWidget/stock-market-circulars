## Summary

BSE has listed two series of Commercial Papers issued by Tata Steel Limited on private placement basis with effect from October 17, 2025. The first series (Scrip Code: 730126) consists of 33,000 units with redemption on November 28, 2025, while the second series (Scrip Code: 730409) consists of 15,000 units with redemption on December 16, 2025. Both carry ICRA A1+ and IND A1+ credit ratings.

## Key Points

- Two Commercial Paper series listed on BSE Debt segment effective October 17, 2025
- Series 1: 33,000 units, Scrip Code 730126 (TSL010925), ISIN INE081A14GC8, redemption November 28, 2025
- Series 2: 15,000 units, Scrip Code 730409 (TSL161025), ISIN INE081A14GH7, redemption December 16, 2025
- Face value and paid-up value: Rs. 5,00,000 per unit for both series
- Issue prices: Rs. 496,519.50 (Series 1) and Rs. 495,077.00 (Series 2)
- Allotment date: October 16, 2025 for both series
- Credit rating: ICRA A1+ and IND A1+ for both instruments
- HDFC Bank Limited acts as Issuing and Paying Agent
- Trading only in dematerialized form with standard denomination of Rs. 5 lakhs and multiples
- Market lot: 1 unit, tick size: 1 paise

## Regulatory Changes

No regulatory changes announced.

## Compliance Requirements

- Trading members must trade these securities only in dematerialized form under the specified ISIN numbers
- Trading must be conducted in standard denomination of Rs. 5 lakhs and multiples thereof
- Tick size of 1 paise must be maintained for these securities

## Important Dates

- **October 16, 2025**: Allotment date for both CP series
- **October 17, 2025**: Listing and admission to dealings on BSE Debt segment
- **November 28, 2025**: Redemption date for Series 1 (Scrip Code 730126)
- **December 16, 2025**: Redemption date for Series 2 (Scrip Code 730409)

## Impact Assessment

This is a routine listing notification with minimal market impact. The listing of Commercial Papers by Tata Steel provides short-term debt instruments for institutional investors in the debt segment. The high credit ratings (ICRA A1+ and IND A1+) reflect strong creditworthiness. This has no direct impact on equity trading or retail investors, as these instruments are traded in large denominations (Rs. 5 lakhs minimum) primarily among institutional participants in the debt market.
