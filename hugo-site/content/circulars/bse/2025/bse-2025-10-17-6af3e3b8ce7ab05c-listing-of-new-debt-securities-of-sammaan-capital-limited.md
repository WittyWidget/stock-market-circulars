## Summary

BSE has listed two new series of non-convertible debentures issued by Sammaan Capital Limited on private placement basis, effective October 17, 2025. The securities comprise 20,000 units each of 5-year and 10-year NCDs with face value of Rs. 1,00,000 per unit, carrying interest rates of 9.15% p.a. and 9.30% p.a. respectively. Both series are rated CRISIL AA/Stable and ICRA AA/Stable.

## Key Points

- Two new NCD series from Sammaan Capital Limited listed on BSE Debt segment
- Series 1: 9.30% NCDs maturing October 16, 2035 (10-year tenor)
- Series 2: 9.15% NCDs maturing October 16, 2030 (5-year tenor)
- Both series: 20,000 units, face value Rs. 1,00,000, market lot of 1
- Date of allotment: October 16, 2025
- Credit ratings: CRISIL AA/Stable and ICRA AA/Stable for both series
- Interest payment frequency: Annual
- No put/call options available

## Regulatory Changes

No regulatory changes introduced. This is a standard listing notification.

## Compliance Requirements

- Trading members must trade these securities only in dematerialized form under the specified ISIN numbers
- Securities to be traded with tick size of 1 paise
- Trading members should refer to the Placement Memorandum available at https://www.bseindia.com/markets/debt/memorandum_data.aspx for additional details
- Clarifications can be obtained from BSE debt department at contact numbers: 22728352/8597/8995/5753/8915

## Important Dates

- **Date of Allotment**: October 16, 2025
- **Listing Date**: October 17, 2025
- **Redemption Date (10-year NCD)**: October 16, 2035
- **Redemption Date (5-year NCD)**: October 16, 2030

## Impact Assessment

Minimal market-wide impact as this is a routine listing of privately placed debt securities. The listing provides liquidity options for investors holding these instruments and expands the debt market offerings on BSE. The high credit ratings (AA/Stable) indicate low credit risk. The interest rates of 9.15%-9.30% reflect prevailing market conditions for AA-rated instruments with 5-10 year tenors. Impact is primarily limited to debt market participants, institutional investors, and existing Sammaan Capital stakeholders.
