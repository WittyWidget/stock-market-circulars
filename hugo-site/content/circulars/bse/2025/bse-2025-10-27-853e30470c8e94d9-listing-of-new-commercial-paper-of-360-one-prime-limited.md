## Summary

360 ONE Prime Limited has listed new commercial paper securities on BSE's Debt segment effective October 27, 2025. The instruments were issued on private placement basis with a face value of Rs. 5,00,000 each, totaling 4,000 units. The commercial paper carries credit ratings of CRISIL A1+ and ICRA A1+, with HDFC Bank Limited acting as the Issuing and Paying agent.

## Key Points

- 4,000 units of commercial paper listed with face value of Rs. 5,00,000 each
- Scrip Code: 730436, Scrip ID: OPL241025
- ISIN Number: INE248U14SK9
- Issue Price: Rs. 4,91,183.00 per unit
- Credit Ratings: CRISIL A1+ and ICRA A1+
- Market Lot: 1 unit
- Trading only in dematerialized form
- Standard denomination: Rs. 5 Lakhs and multiples thereof
- Tick size: 1 paise
- HDFC Bank Limited is the Issuing and Paying agent

## Regulatory Changes

No regulatory changes introduced. This is a standard listing notification for new debt securities.

## Compliance Requirements

- Trading members must trade these securities only in dematerialized form under ISIN INE248U14SK9
- Trading must be conducted in standard denomination of Rs. 5 Lakhs and multiples thereof
- Members must use the tick size of 1 paise for trading
- For clarifications, trading members should contact BSE debt department on 22728352/8597/8995/5753/8915

## Important Dates

- **Date of Allotment**: October 24, 2025
- **Listing Date**: October 27, 2025
- **Date of Redemption**: January 23, 2026
- **Notice Date**: October 27, 2025

## Impact Assessment

This listing has minimal market impact as it represents a routine addition of short-term debt instruments to the BSE Debt segment. The commercial paper has a tenure of approximately 3 months (91 days) and is rated A1+ by both CRISIL and ICRA, indicating highest safety for short-term instruments. The listing provides additional liquidity options for debt market participants interested in 360 ONE Prime Limited's securities. The discount of approximately 1.76% (issue price Rs. 4,91,183 versus face value Rs. 5,00,000) reflects prevailing short-term money market rates.
