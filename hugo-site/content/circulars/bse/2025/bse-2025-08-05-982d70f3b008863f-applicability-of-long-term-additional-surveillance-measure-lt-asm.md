## Summary

BSE has released the list of securities subject to Long Term Additional Surveillance Measure (LT-ASM) framework effective August 6, 2025. Two securities have been newly shortlisted: Avi Products India Ltd and Mehai Technology Ltd. No securities are moving between ASM stages or exiting the framework.

## Key Points

- Two new securities added to LT-ASM framework: Avi Products India Ltd (523896) and Mehai Technology Ltd (540730)
- No securities moving to higher or lower ASM stages
- No securities moving out of LT-ASM framework
- Consolidated list shows multiple securities across different ASM stages (Stage I primarily)
- Framework includes provisions for T+0 scrips and various exit criteria

## Regulatory Changes

- Implementation of LT-ASM framework for identified securities
- Enhanced surveillance measures for securities showing irregular price movements or other concerns
- Securities may move between different ASM stages based on performance
- Exit provisions for securities meeting criteria for Trade for Trade, GSM, ESM, or IBC frameworks

## Compliance Requirements

- Trading members must comply with enhanced surveillance requirements for listed securities
- Increased margin requirements and position limits may apply
- Enhanced disclosure and monitoring obligations for affected securities
- Compliance with specific trading restrictions as applicable to ASM framework

## Important Dates

- **Effective Date**: August 6, 2025 - New LT-ASM framework implementation
- All changes to surveillance measures become applicable from this date

## Impact Assessment

- **Market Impact**: Limited as only two new securities added to surveillance framework
- **Trading Impact**: Enhanced surveillance may reduce speculative trading in affected securities
- **Investor Impact**: Increased margin requirements and position monitoring for investors in these securities
- **Operational Impact**: Trading members need to update systems and processes for surveillance compliance
