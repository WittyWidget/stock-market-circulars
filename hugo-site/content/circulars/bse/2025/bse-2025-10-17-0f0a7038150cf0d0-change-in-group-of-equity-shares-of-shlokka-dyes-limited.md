## Summary

BSE has announced that the equity shares of SHLOKKA DYES LIMITED (Scrip Code: 544582) will be transferred from the Trade for Trade segment (MT Group) to the Rolling segment (M Group) effective Tuesday, November 04, 2025. This change follows Exchange Notice No. 220251016-39 dated October 16, 2025.

## Key Points

- Company: SHLOKKA DYES LIMITED
- Scrip Code: 544582
- Current Segment: Trade for Trade segment (MT Group)
- New Segment: Rolling segment (M Group)
- Effective Date: Tuesday, November 04, 2025
- Segment: SME
- Notice Number: 20251017-2
- Contact: Mr. Anurag Jain, Tel: 022-2272 8822

## Regulatory Changes

The trading mechanism for SHLOKKA DYES LIMITED will change from Trade for Trade (T2T) to Rolling settlement. In T2T segment, all trades result in compulsory delivery with no intraday trading allowed. The move to Rolling segment allows normal trading with intraday positions permitted.

## Compliance Requirements

- Trading Members must note the segment change and update their systems accordingly
- Trading Members should contact Mr. Anurag Jain (Assistant Vice President) for further details or clarifications
- All trades from November 04, 2025 onwards will be under M Group (Rolling segment) rules

## Important Dates

- **October 16, 2025**: Initial notice issued (Notice No. 220251016-39)
- **October 17, 2025**: Confirmation notice issued
- **November 04, 2025**: Effective date for segment transfer

## Impact Assessment

**Trading Impact**: The shift from T2T to Rolling segment increases liquidity and trading flexibility for SHLOKKA DYES LIMITED shares. Investors will now be able to conduct intraday trading, which was previously restricted. This typically indicates improved compliance or regulatory standing of the company.

**Market Participants**: Trading members and brokers need to update their systems and inform clients about the changed trading mechanism. The move to M Group suggests the company has met requirements to exit the more restrictive T2T framework.

**Investor Impact**: Medium positive impact for investors as the stock becomes more liquid and tradeable with reduced restrictions.
