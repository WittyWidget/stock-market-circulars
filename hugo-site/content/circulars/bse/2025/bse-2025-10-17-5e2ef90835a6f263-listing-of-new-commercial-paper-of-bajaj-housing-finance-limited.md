## Summary

BSE has listed new Commercial Paper securities issued by Bajaj Housing Finance Limited on private placement basis. The securities are admitted to dealings on the BSE Debt segment with effect from October 17, 2025. The issue consists of 15,000 units with a face value of Rs. 5,00,000 each, issued at Rs. 492,606.50 per unit, maturing on January 15, 2026.

## Key Points

- **Issuer**: Bajaj Housing Finance Limited
- **Securities Type**: Commercial Paper (Private Placement)
- **Quantity**: 15,000 units
- **Face Value**: Rs. 5,00,000 per unit
- **Issue Price**: Rs. 492,606.50 per unit
- **Scrip Code**: 730415
- **Scrip ID**: BHFL161025
- **Detail Name**: BHFL-15-01-26-CP
- **ISIN**: INE377Y14BT5
- **Credit Rating**: CRISIL A1+, IND A1+
- **Market Lot**: 1 unit
- **Tick Size**: 1 paise
- **Issuing and Paying Agent**: Central Bank of India

## Regulatory Changes

No regulatory changes introduced. This is a standard listing notification for debt securities.

## Compliance Requirements

- Trading members must trade these securities only in dematerialised form under ISIN INE377Y14BT5
- Trading shall take place in standard denomination of Rs. 5 lakhs and multiples thereof
- Securities will be traded on the BSE Debt segment
- For clarifications, trading members should contact the debt department at 22728352/8597/8995/5753/8915

## Important Dates

- **Notice Date**: October 17, 2025
- **Listing Effective Date**: October 17, 2025
- **Date of Allotment**: October 16, 2025
- **Redemption Date**: January 15, 2026

## Impact Assessment

**Market Impact**: Minimal. This is a routine commercial paper listing in the debt segment with a short maturity period of approximately 3 months. The high credit ratings (CRISIL A1+ and IND A1+) indicate strong creditworthiness.

**Operational Impact**: Limited to debt market participants and investors interested in short-term debt instruments. The standard denomination of Rs. 5 lakhs makes it accessible to institutional investors and high net-worth individuals. Trading members dealing in debt securities need to update their systems with the new scrip details.
