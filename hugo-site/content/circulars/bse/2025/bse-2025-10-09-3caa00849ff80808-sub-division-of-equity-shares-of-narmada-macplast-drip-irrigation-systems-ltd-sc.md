## Summary

BSE has announced the sub-division of equity shares of Narmada Macplast Drip Irrigation Systems Ltd (Scrip Code: 517431). Each existing equity share with a face value of Rs. 10/- will be split into five equity shares with a face value of Rs. 2/- each. The record date for this subdivision is October 18, 2025, with the new denomination effective from October 17, 2025.

## Key Points

- Company: Narmada Macplast Drip Irrigation Systems Ltd
- Scrip Code: 517431
- Subdivision Ratio: 1:5 (One share of Rs. 10/- into Five shares of Rs. 2/-)
- Record Date: October 18, 2025
- Effective Date: October 17, 2025 (W.E.F.)
- Segment: Equity - Dematerialised Securities â€“ Rolling Settlement
- Notice Number: DR-738/2025-2026
- Current ISIN (Rs. 10/- paid up): INE060D01010 (will become invalid)
- New ISIN for Rs. 2/- paid up: To be informed separately

## Regulatory Changes

No regulatory framework changes. This is a standard corporate action notification for stock subdivision.

## Compliance Requirements

- Trading members must note that ISIN No. INE060D01010 (Rs. 10/- paid up) will not be valid for transactions on the Exchange on or after October 17, 2025
- Market participants must await separate notice for new ISIN Number for Rs. 2/- paid up shares
- All trades from October 17, 2025 onwards must use the new ISIN once announced

## Important Dates

- **October 9, 2025**: Circular issued
- **October 17, 2025**: Effective date for subdivision; old ISIN becomes invalid for trading
- **October 18, 2025**: Record date for subdivision eligibility

## Impact Assessment

**Shareholder Impact**: Existing shareholders will receive 5 shares for every 1 share held as of the record date. Total investment value remains unchanged, but number of shares increases proportionally.

**Trading Impact**: Stock subdivision typically improves liquidity and makes shares more accessible to retail investors by reducing per-share price. Trading will transition to new ISIN from October 17, 2025.

**Operational Impact**: Investors and trading members must ensure they are aware of ISIN changes to avoid transaction failures. The old ISIN will be deactivated for trading purposes from the effective date.
