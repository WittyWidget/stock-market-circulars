## Summary

BSE has listed and admitted new debt securities issued by Bajaj Housing Finance Limited on private placement basis to trading on the BSE Debt segment effective October 14, 2025. The issuance consists of 100,000 securities with CRISIL AAA/stable rating, carrying 6.95% annual interest rate and maturing on January 28, 2028.

## Key Points

- Issuer: Bajaj Housing Finance Limited
- Quantity: 100,000 securities
- Face Value: Rs. 100,000 per unit
- Issue Price: Rs. 100,009.27 per unit
- Total Issue Size: Approximately Rs. 1,000 crore
- Scrip Code: 977204
- Scrip ID: 695BHFL28
- ISIN: INE377Y07615
- Credit Rating: CRISIL AAA/stable
- Interest Rate: 6.95% per annum (paid annually)
- Allotment Date: October 13, 2025
- Maturity Date: January 28, 2028
- Market Lot: 1 unit
- Tick Size: 1 paise
- Trading Mode: Dematerialized form only
- Put/Call Option: Not applicable

## Regulatory Changes

No regulatory changes. This is a standard listing notification.

## Compliance Requirements

- Trading members must trade these securities only in dematerialized form under ISIN INE377Y07615
- Trading members should refer to the Placement Memorandum available at https://www.bseindia.com/markets/debt/memorandum_data.aspx for further details
- For clarifications, trading members may contact BSE debt department at 22728352/8597/8995/5753/8915

## Important Dates

- **October 13, 2025**: Date of Allotment
- **October 14, 2025**: Effective date of listing and trading commencement
- **January 28, 2028**: Redemption date
- **Annual**: Interest payment frequency

## Impact Assessment

**Market Impact**: Minimal. This is a routine private placement debt listing with no impact on equity markets or broader trading activities.

**Operational Impact**: Low. Standard debt security listing that adds to the BSE Debt segment inventory. The AAA rating indicates high credit quality, making it suitable for institutional investors seeking fixed-income securities.

**Investor Impact**: Relevant only for debt market participants and institutional investors interested in high-rated corporate debt with approximately 2.3-year tenure.
