## Summary

BSE has published the list of securities for Demat Auction under Settlement Number 736 dated October 16, 2025. The auction includes a wide range of securities from various companies across different sectors, with quantities ranging from 1 share to 100,000 shares depending on the scrip.

## Key Points

- Settlement Number: 736
- Date: October 16, 2025
- Total securities listed: Approximately 140+ scrips
- Quantity range: 1 to 100,000 shares per scrip
- Includes major companies like Reliance Industries (10 shares), ITC Ltd (26 shares), Bajaj Finance (33 shares), and M&M (507 shares)
- Highest quantity: Vantage Know (100,000 shares), Enbee Trade (98,000 shares)
- Covers diverse sectors including banking, auto, pharma, IT, energy, and infrastructure

## Regulatory Changes

No regulatory changes are introduced in this circular. This is a standard operational auction notice.

## Compliance Requirements

- Parties with outstanding settlement obligations for Settlement No. 736 must participate in the demat auction
- Members must ensure delivery of securities or fulfillment of auction obligations as per BSE settlement rules
- Auction participants must comply with BSE's demat auction procedures and timelines

## Important Dates

- Settlement Date: October 16, 2025
- Settlement Number: 736
- Auction execution date: As per BSE's standard auction schedule for the settlement

## Impact Assessment

**Market Impact:** Medium - The auction affects a large number of securities across various sectors. While individual quantities for most scrips are relatively small, the breadth of securities involved indicates widespread settlement obligations.

**Operational Impact:** Medium - Parties with pending obligations in these securities must arrange for auction participation or face buy-in consequences. The highest impact is on securities with large quantities like Vantage Know (100,000), Enbee Trade (98,000), Motherson Sumi (6,797), Reliance Power (6,700), and Nippon Life (14,613).

**Investor Impact:** Limited to specific parties with settlement failures. General investors are not directly affected unless they are counterparties to the failed settlements.
