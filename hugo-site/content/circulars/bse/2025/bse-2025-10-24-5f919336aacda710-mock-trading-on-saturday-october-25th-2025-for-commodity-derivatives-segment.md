## Summary

BSE has scheduled a mock trading session for the Commodity Derivatives segment on Saturday, October 25, 2025. Trading members using third-party platforms or in-house systems can test their trading applications during this session. The mock session will run from 2:00 PM to 5:00 PM with login starting at 1:15 PM. No new BOLT TWS release is planned. Trades from this session will not create any margin, pay-in, pay-out obligations or legal rights and liabilities.

## Key Points

- Mock trading session scheduled for Commodity Derivatives segment on October 25, 2025
- Testing opportunity for third-party trading platforms and in-house systems using ETI APIs
- Session will simulate exceptional market conditions including risk-reduction mode and trading halt
- No new BOLT TWS release planned for this session
- Trades will not attract any real obligations or create rights/liabilities
- Members requested to provide feedback by 6:00 PM on the session day
- Active participation from all members is encouraged

## Regulatory Changes

No regulatory changes. This is a routine mock trading session.

## Compliance Requirements

- Trading members are encouraged to actively participate in the mock session
- Members must submit feedback by 6:00 PM on October 25, 2025
- Contact BSE Helpdesk (022-45720400/600, 022-69158500) or Tech Support (022-22728053) for queries
- Email feedback to bsehelp@bseindia.com or bse.tech@bseindia.com

## Important Dates

- **October 25, 2025**: Mock Trading Session
  - 1:15 PM - 2:00 PM: Log-in window
  - 2:00 PM - 5:00 PM: Continuous trading
  - 5:00 PM - 5:10 PM: Trade modification
  - By 6:00 PM: Feedback submission deadline

## Impact Assessment

**Market Impact**: None. This is a mock trading session with no real market transactions or obligations.

**Operational Impact**: Low. Provides testing opportunity for trading members to validate their systems and familiarize themselves with exceptional market conditions. Members using third-party or in-house trading platforms can use this session to ensure system readiness and test various functionalities in a controlled environment.
