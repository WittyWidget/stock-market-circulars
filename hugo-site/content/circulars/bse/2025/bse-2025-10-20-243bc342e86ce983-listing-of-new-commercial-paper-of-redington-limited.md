## Summary

BSE has listed two series of Commercial Paper issued by Redington Limited on private placement basis, effective October 20, 2025. The first series comprises 4,000 units of Rs. 5 lakhs each (total Rs. 200 crores) maturing on December 16, 2025. The second series comprises 2,000 units of Rs. 5 lakhs each (total Rs. 100 crores) maturing on January 9, 2026. Both series carry CRISIL A1+ and ICRA A1+ ratings.

## Key Points

- Series 1: 4,000 units of Commercial Paper with face value Rs. 5,00,000 each (Scrip Code: 730417, ISIN: INE891D14A87)
- Series 1 issue price: Rs. 4,95,100.50, allotted on October 17, 2025, redemption on December 16, 2025
- Series 2: 2,000 units of Commercial Paper with face value Rs. 5,00,000 each (Scrip Code: 730418, ISIN: INE891D14A95)
- Series 2 issue price: Rs. 4,93,100.50, allotted on October 17, 2025, redemption on January 9, 2026
- Both series rated CRISIL A1+ and ICRA A1+
- HDFC Bank Ltd acts as Issuing and Paying Agent
- Trading only in dematerialized form
- Standard denomination: Rs. 5 lakhs and multiples thereof
- Market lot: 1 unit
- Tick size: 1 paise

## Regulatory Changes

No regulatory changes introduced. This is a standard listing notification.

## Compliance Requirements

- Trading members must trade these securities only in dematerialized form under the specified ISIN numbers
- Trading must be conducted in standard denomination of Rs. 5 lakhs and multiples thereof
- Members should contact BSE debt department (22728352/8597/8995/5753/8915) for any clarifications

## Important Dates

- **Allotment Date**: October 17, 2025 (both series)
- **Listing Date**: October 20, 2025 (both series)
- **Redemption Date (Series 1)**: December 16, 2025
- **Redemption Date (Series 2)**: January 9, 2026

## Impact Assessment

This is a routine commercial paper listing with minimal market impact. The instruments are short-term debt securities with tenures of approximately 60-84 days. The high credit ratings (A1+) indicate low credit risk. Impact is limited to debt market participants interested in short-term corporate paper. No effect on equity markets or broader trading operations. The total issuance of Rs. 300 crores represents standard corporate treasury management activity.
