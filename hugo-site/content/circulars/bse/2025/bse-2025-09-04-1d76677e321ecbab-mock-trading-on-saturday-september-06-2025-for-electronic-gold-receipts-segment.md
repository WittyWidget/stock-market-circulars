## Summary

BSE has scheduled a mock trading session for Electronic Gold Receipts (EGR) segment on Saturday, September 06, 2025. This session is designed for testing and familiarization purposes, allowing trading members to test their systems and trading applications. The mock trading will not create any financial obligations or rights and liabilities.

## Key Points

- Mock trading session scheduled for EGR segment on September 06, 2025
- New BOLT Pro TWS version 12.03 will be released
- Members can choose between Mock Trading Session or UAT environment for regulatory compliance
- Third-party trading platforms and in-house systems can test various functionalities
- Trades from mock session will not attract margin obligations or settlement obligations
- Active participation and feedback requested from all members

## Regulatory Changes

No regulatory changes. This is a testing session in accordance with existing SEBI circular SEBI/HO/MRD1/DSAP/CIR/P/2020/234 dated November 24, 2020.

## Compliance Requirements

- Trading members may participate to fulfill regulatory requirements for system testing
- Members using third-party platforms or in-house systems should utilize this opportunity for testing
- Feedback must be provided by 4:30 PM on the mock trading day
- Reference to Exchange Notice No 20201201-22 regarding testing of trading and risk management software

## Important Dates

- **Mock Trading Date**: September 06, 2025 (Saturday)
- **Trading Sessions Schedule**:
  - Log-in: 10:15 AM - 11:00 AM
  - Pre-Open Order Entry: 11:00 AM - 11:08 AM
  - Matching Period: 11:08 AM - 11:15 AM
  - Continuous Trading: 11:15 AM - 3:30 PM
  - Block Deal Window: 12:00 PM - 12:15 PM
  - Closing: 3:30 PM - 3:40 PM
  - Post-closing: 3:40 PM - 3:50 PM
- **Feedback Deadline**: 4:30 PM on September 06, 2025

## Impact Assessment

Minimal market impact as this is a mock trading session with no actual financial transactions. The session provides opportunity for members to test systems, familiarize with new BOLT Pro version, and ensure compliance with testing requirements. No impact on actual EGR trading or settlement processes.
