## Summary

BSE has issued updates regarding the Short Term Additional Surveillance Measure (ST-ASM) framework effective August 11, 2025. The circular details securities being newly included in ST-ASM, those moving out of the framework, and consolidated listings of all securities under various ST-ASM stages.

## Key Points

- 2 new securities added to Short Term 5/15/30 Days ASM Framework
- 9 securities moving out of ST-ASM framework due to inclusion in other surveillance measures
- No securities moving to higher or lower ASM stages within ST-ASM framework
- Consolidated list includes 14+ securities across different ST-ASM stages
- Special markings indicate SME scrips (@) and reasons for framework changes

## Regulatory Changes

- Implementation of Short Term ASM framework changes effective August 11, 2025
- Securities transition between different surveillance frameworks (LT-ASM, Trade for Trade, GSM, ESM, Pledge)
- Stage-wise classification system for ST-ASM securities

## Compliance Requirements

- Market participants must comply with ST-ASM trading restrictions for listed securities
- Enhanced surveillance measures apply to specified securities
- Separate treatment for SME scrips under ST-ASM framework

## Important Dates

- **August 11, 2025**: Effective date for all ST-ASM framework changes

## Impact Assessment

**New Inclusions**: Clinitech Laboratory Ltd and Patels Airtemp India Ltd face enhanced surveillance measures which may impact trading liquidity and investor participation.

**Framework Exits**: 9 securities moving out of ST-ASM into other surveillance frameworks (LT-ASM, Trade for Trade, GSM, ESM) will experience different trading restrictions and compliance requirements.

**Market Operations**: Changes affect trading patterns, margin requirements, and settlement procedures for the specified securities, requiring broker and investor awareness of new surveillance parameters.
