## Summary

The National Stock Exchange has announced a list of securities that will be moved to Stage I of the Graded Surveillance Measure (GSM) framework. This measure is designed to enhance market integrity through increased surveillance of securities exhibiting specific price-volume patterns.

## Key Points

- Securities identified for inclusion in GSM Stage I based on surveillance criteria
- Enhanced monitoring and trading restrictions will apply to listed securities
- Price band of 5% or lower applicable from the effective date
- Trade-for-trade settlement mandatory for all GSM securities
- Additional surveillance deposit of 100% required for these securities

## Regulatory Changes

Securities under GSM Stage I will be subject to:
- Restricted price bands (5% or lower)
- Mandatory trade-for-trade settlement
- Enhanced surveillance and monitoring
- Additional margin requirements
- Periodic review for stage progression or exit

## Compliance Requirements

- Trading members must ensure compliance with applicable price bands
- All trades must be settled on trade-for-trade basis
- Members to collect additional surveillance deposit from clients
- Risk management systems to be updated for GSM securities
- Regular monitoring of client positions in GSM securities

## Important Dates

- Circular Date: September 2, 2025
- Effective Date: As specified in the circular
- Review Period: Quarterly review for stage transition

## Impact Assessment

The inclusion of securities in GSM Stage I will impact market participants through restricted trading parameters, increased margin requirements, and settlement restrictions. This may result in reduced liquidity and increased compliance obligations for trading members dealing in these securities.
