## Summary

NSE announces a change in trading holiday for September 2025. The previously declared holiday on September 05, 2025 has been cancelled, and September 08, 2025 is now declared as a public holiday under the Negotiable Instruments Act for Id-E-Milad celebration. This change affects currency derivatives trading and settlement schedules.

## Key Points

- Original trading holiday September 05, 2025 cancelled
- New trading holiday declared for September 08, 2025
- September 05, 2025 will now be a normal trading day with regular market timings
- Change applies to currency derivatives segment
- Members must download updated contract files before trading on September 05, 2025

## Regulatory Changes

- Maharashtra Government declared September 08, 2025 as public holiday under Section 25 of Negotiable Instruments Act, 1881
- Previous holiday declaration for September 05, 2025 has been cancelled
- RBI confirms no transactions and settlements in government securities, forex and money markets on September 08, 2025

## Compliance Requirements

- Members advised to download updated cd_contract.gz and cd_spd_contract.gz files from NSE extranet before trading on September 05, 2025
- Files available at path: /cdsftp/cdscommon on NSE extranet
- Settlement schedules to be communicated separately by respective clearing corporations

## Important Dates

- **September 05, 2025**: Normal trading day (previously declared holiday, now operational)
- **September 08, 2025**: New trading holiday for Id-E-Milad
- **September 09, 2025**: Settlement of outstanding transactions due on September 08 will be postponed to this date

## Impact Assessment

- Currency derivatives market will operate normally on September 05, 2025
- All outstanding settlements due on September 08, 2025 will be postponed to September 09, 2025
- Government securities auction settlement conducted on September 04, 2025 will settle on September 09, 2025
- SDF and MSF facilities under LAF will be available as per revised holiday schedule
- Minimal operational disruption as this is a holiday rescheduling rather than additional closure
