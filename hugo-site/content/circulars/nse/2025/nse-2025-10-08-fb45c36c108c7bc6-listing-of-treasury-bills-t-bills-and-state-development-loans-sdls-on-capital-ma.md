## Summary

NSE has admitted 10 government securities for trading on the capital market segment effective October 10, 2025. The listing includes 3 Treasury Bills (T-bills) with varying maturities and 7 State Development Loans (SDLs) from states including Jammu & Kashmir, Gujarat, Andhra Pradesh, Maharashtra, and Mizoram. All securities will trade with a lot size of 100 units.

## Key Points

- 3 Treasury Bills introduced: 91-day (maturing Jan 2026), 182-day (maturing Apr 2026), and 364-day (maturing Oct 2026)
- 7 State Development Loans from 5 states with coupon rates ranging from 7.06% to 7.39%
- All securities have uniform lot size of 100 units
- Securities identified by designated codes for trading purposes
- Trading commences October 10, 2025

## Regulatory Framework

This listing is issued pursuant to:
- Regulation 3.1.1 of NSE (Capital Market) Trading Regulations Part A - admission of securities to dealings
- Regulation 2.5.5 of NSE (Capital Market) Trading Regulations Part A - lot size specifications

## Trading Parameters

- **Market Type**: TB (Treasury Bills) and SG (State Government securities)
- **Lot Size**: 100 units for all securities
- **Identification**: Securities identified by designated codes on trading system

## Important Dates

- **Circular Date**: October 8, 2025
- **Effective Date**: October 10, 2025

## Impact Assessment

This circular provides routine expansion of tradable government securities on NSE's capital market segment. The addition of these T-bills and SDLs offers investors more options in government debt instruments with varying maturities (short-term T-bills to long-term SDLs maturing up to 2042). The impact is primarily relevant to institutional investors, debt market participants, and portfolio managers seeking government-backed fixed-income securities. No impact on equity markets or retail investors.
