## Summary

NSE Clearing Limited has announced the updated Volatility Scan Range (VSR) percentages for commodity option contracts applicable for September 2025. The VSR is a risk management parameter used for margin calculations on option contracts in the commodity derivatives segment.

## Key Points

- VSR update applies to six major commodities and their variants
- COPPER: 5% VSR (no change indicated)
- GOLD: 4% VSR (lowest among listed commodities)
- SILVER: 6% VSR
- ZINC: 6% VSR
- CRUDEOIL: 33% VSR (highest due to energy commodity volatility)
- NATURALGAS: 6% VSR
- Continues implementation of SEBI circular dated January 11, 2021

## Regulatory Changes

This is a routine monthly review and update of VSR parameters as per SEBI regulations. The VSR percentages reflect the expected volatility and risk assessment for each commodity category.

## Compliance Requirements

- All NSE members must take note of the updated VSR percentages
- VSR parameters will be applied to margin calculations for September 2025 option contracts
- Members should adjust their risk management systems accordingly

## Important Dates

- **Effective Date**: September 2025 (for option contracts in that month)
- **Circular Date**: August 14, 2025
- **Reference Period**: Monthly review cycle

## Impact Assessment

The updated VSR percentages will directly impact margin requirements for commodity option trading. CRUDEOIL maintains the highest VSR at 33%, reflecting the inherent volatility in energy markets, while GOLD has the lowest at 4%, indicating relatively stable price movements. These parameters affect trader capital requirements and position limits in the commodity derivatives segment.
