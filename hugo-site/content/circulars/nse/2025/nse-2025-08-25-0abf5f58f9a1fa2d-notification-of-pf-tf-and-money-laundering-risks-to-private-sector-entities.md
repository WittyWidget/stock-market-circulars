## Summary

NSE has communicated SEBI's notification regarding FATF's report on Complex Proliferation Financing and Sanctions Evasion Schemes (CPFSES) released on June 20, 2025. The circular highlights specific high-risk jurisdictions - Democratic People's Republic of Korea (DPRK) and Pakistan - that require enhanced monitoring and risk assessment by intermediaries.

## Key Points

- FATF released "Complex Proliferation Financing and Sanctions Evasion Schemes" report on June 20, 2025
- Report identifies strategies, typologies and lessons from multiple jurisdictions for sanctions evasion
- DPRK identified as high-risk due to UN Security Council sanctions
- Pakistan flagged based on Indian PF-linked investigations and sanctions evasion by state-owned enterprises
- Intermediaries must update Institutional Risk Assessments under Rule 9(13) of Prevention of Money Laundering Rules

## Regulatory Changes

- Enhanced focus on proliferation financing risks from specific jurisdictions
- Updated risk assessment requirements for intermediaries dealing with DPRK and Pakistan-related transactions
- Strengthened compliance mechanisms for sanctions evasion prevention

## Compliance Requirements

- Update internal risk policies to account for identified high-risk jurisdictions
- Ensure Institutional Risk Assessments comply with Rule 9(13) of Prevention of Money Laundering Rules
- Implement enhanced monitoring for transactions involving DPRK and Pakistan
- Regular monitoring of SEBI website for updates on this matter

## Important Dates

- FATF report release date: June 20, 2025
- Office memorandum date: July 31, 2025
- NSE circular date: August 25, 2025

## Impact Assessment

- All market intermediaries must review and update their risk assessment frameworks
- Enhanced due diligence requirements for transactions involving specified high-risk jurisdictions
- Potential operational impact on compliance processes and monitoring systems
- Critical for maintaining regulatory compliance and avoiding sanctions violations
