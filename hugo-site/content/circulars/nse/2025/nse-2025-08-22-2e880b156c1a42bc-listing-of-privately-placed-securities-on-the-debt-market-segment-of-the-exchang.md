## Summary

NSE has admitted commercial papers from 9 companies to trading on its debt market segment effective August 22, 2025. The listings include papers from major financial services companies like IIFL Finance, Godrej Industries, Motilal Oswal Financial Services, and others with redemption dates primarily in November 2025.

## Key Points

- 10 commercial paper securities listed across 9 companies
- All securities have face value of ₹5,00,000 each
- Total securities quantity: 84,000 units
- Allotment dates: August 21-22, 2025
- Redemption dates: November 18-21, 2025
- Issue prices range from ₹489,624 to ₹492,703.5 per unit

## Listed Securities Details

**IIFL Finance Limited**: 5,000 securities, ISIN INE530B14EV7, redemption Nov 20, 2025
**Godrej Industries Limited**: 1,500 securities, ISIN INE233A143Q5, redemption Nov 20, 2025
**Motilal Oswal Financial Services**: 3,000 securities, ISIN INE338I14KW5, redemption Nov 20, 2025
**IIFL Capital Services**: 4,000 securities, ISIN INE489L14587, redemption Nov 20, 2025
**PNB Housing Finance**: 26,000 securities, ISIN INE572E14KA4, redemption Nov 20, 2025
**Kisetsu Saison Finance**: 10,000 securities (2 series), ISINs INE0DZE14230 & INE0DZE14248
**DCM Shriram Limited**: 4,000 securities, ISIN INE499A14DD7, redemption Nov 20, 2025
**Kotak Securities**: 10,500 securities, ISIN INE028E14SH5, redemption Nov 21, 2025
**Hindustan Zinc**: 21,000 securities, ISIN INE267A14887, redemption Nov 21, 2025

## Regulatory Changes

None specified - standard listing procedure under Regulation 3.1.1 of NSE Debt Market Trading Regulations.

## Compliance Requirements

No specific compliance requirements mentioned for market participants.

## Important Dates

- **Effective Date**: August 22, 2025
- **Allotment Dates**: August 21-22, 2025
- **Redemption Dates**: November 18-21, 2025

## Impact Assessment

Moderate positive impact on debt market liquidity with addition of short-term investment options. The listings provide investors with commercial paper instruments from established companies across financial services, manufacturing, and commodity sectors. Total value of approximately ₹42 crores adds to available debt market instruments.
