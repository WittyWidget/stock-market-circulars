## Summary

NSE has announced the listing of 12 new government securities on its capital market segment effective August 22, 2025. The securities include 3 Treasury Bills with tenures of 91, 182, and 364 days, and 9 State Development Loans from various states including Bihar, Tamil Nadu, Gujarat, Uttar Pradesh, Goa, West Bengal, and others with coupon rates ranging from 6.95% to 7.49%.

## Key Points

- 12 new government securities to be listed on NSE capital market segment
- Includes 3 Treasury Bills (91D, 182D, 364D) and 9 State Development Loans
- All securities have a uniform lot size of 100
- Trading to commence from August 22, 2025
- Securities identified by designated codes for trading purposes
- SDL coupon rates range from 6.95% (Odisha) to 7.49% (Kerala)
- Maturity periods span from November 2025 to August 2056

## Regulatory Changes

No regulatory changes introduced. This is a routine listing notification under existing NSE Capital Market Trading Regulations Part A, specifically Regulation 3.1.1 for security admission and Regulation 2.5.5 for lot size specifications.

## Compliance Requirements

- Trading members must use designated security codes for transactions
- All trading must be conducted in specified lot sizes of 100 units
- Securities must be identified only by their designated codes on the trading system

## Important Dates

- **August 22, 2025**: Effective date for listing and trading commencement
- **Maturity dates range**: November 20, 2025 (shortest) to August 20, 2056 (longest)

## Impact Assessment

This listing expands the government securities market on NSE, providing additional investment options for institutional investors and market participants. The inclusion of both short-term T-bills and long-term SDLs offers diverse maturity profiles for portfolio management. The uniform lot size of 100 maintains consistency in trading parameters across all listed government securities.
