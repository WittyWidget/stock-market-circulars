## Summary

NSE Clearing Limited has announced the implementation of additional exposure margin at 15% on equity derivatives for securities where the top 10 clients account for more than 20% of Market Wide Position Limit (MWPL). This measure aims to enhance risk management in the derivatives segment.

## Key Points

- Additional exposure margin of 15% to be levied on specific securities in equity derivatives segment
- Applies to securities where top 10 clients hold more than 20% of MWPL
- Higher of additional exposure margin or additional surveillance margin will be applied where both are applicable
- Securities identified based on 3-month rolling data with monthly review
- RBL Bank Limited (RBLBANK) is currently the only security listed under this framework

## Regulatory Changes

- Introduction of concentration-based additional margin framework
- Monthly review mechanism for securities under this framework
- Integration with existing additional surveillance margin structure

## Compliance Requirements

- Trading members must ensure adequate margin coverage for positions in affected securities
- Risk management systems need to be updated to incorporate the new margin requirements
- Members should monitor client concentration in MWPL-affected securities

## Important Dates

- **Effective Date**: August 29, 2025 (immediately after expiry of August 2025 contracts)
- **Review Frequency**: Monthly basis
- **Data Period**: 3-month rolling data for identification

## Impact Assessment

- Increased trading costs for derivatives positions in affected securities
- Enhanced risk management through concentration-based margin requirements
- Potential reduction in speculative activity in securities with high client concentration
- Market participants need to adjust position sizing and capital allocation strategies
- Currently affects RBL Bank Limited derivatives trading
