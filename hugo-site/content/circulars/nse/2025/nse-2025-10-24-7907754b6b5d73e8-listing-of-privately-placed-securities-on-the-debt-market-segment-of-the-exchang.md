## Summary

NSE has admitted privately placed commercial papers from multiple companies to trading on the Debt Market Segment effective October 24, 2025. The circular lists 8 fresh issue commercial papers and 1 re-issue, with a total value exceeding Rs. 2,100 crore across various maturities ranging from January 2026 to September 2026.

## Key Points

- **Fresh Issues**: 8 commercial papers from 7 companies totaling 34,800 securities
- **Re-Issues**: 1 commercial paper from Mahindra & Mahindra Financial Services Limited totaling 10,000 securities
- **Face Value**: All securities have a face value of Rs. 5,00,000 per unit
- **Allotment Dates**: October 23-24, 2025
- **Maturity Range**: January 2026 to September 2026
- **Total Aggregate Value**: Approximately Rs. 2,240 crore

## Regulatory Changes

No regulatory changes introduced. This circular implements existing provisions under Regulation 3.1.1 of the National Stock Exchange Debt Market (Trading) Regulations.

## Compliance Requirements

- Exchange members are notified of the admission of these securities to trading
- Securities are available for trading on the Debt Market Segment from October 24, 2025
- Designated security codes as specified in the annexure must be used for trading

## Important Dates

- **Effective Date**: October 24, 2025 - Securities admitted to dealings
- **Allotment Dates**: October 23-24, 2025
- **Redemption Dates**: Range from January 19, 2026 to September 25, 2026

## Impact Assessment

**Market Impact**: Minimal. This is a routine administrative notification for debt market participants. The listing of commercial papers provides additional short-term debt instruments for institutional investors and does not directly impact equity market operations or retail investors. The securities are privately placed and traded on the wholesale debt market segment.
