## Summary

NSE has announced adjustments to Futures and Options contracts in INOX WIND LIMITED (INOXWIND) following the company's rights issue announcement. The adjustment is necessitated by a 5:78 rights issue at ₹120 per equity share, with a total issue size of ₹1,249.33 crores. All existing F&O contracts will be adjusted to maintain economic equivalence and prevent arbitrage opportunities.

## Key Points

- Rights issue ratio of 5:78 (5 new shares for every 78 existing shares)
- Issue price set at ₹120 per equity share
- Total issue size of ₹1,249.33 crores covering 10,41,10,712 equity shares
- Strike prices and lot sizes of existing options contracts will be proportionally adjusted
- Adjustments follow SEBI prescribed guidelines for corporate actions in derivatives
- MUFG Intime India Private Limited appointed as registrar for the rights issue

## Regulatory Changes

The adjustment framework follows established SEBI guidelines for handling corporate actions in derivatives markets. Contract specifications including strike prices and lot sizes will be modified to reflect the rights issue impact while maintaining the economic value of existing positions.

## Compliance Requirements

- Market participants must review and understand the contract adjustments
- Traders should monitor their adjusted positions post-implementation
- All adjustments will be implemented automatically by the exchange
- Detailed adjustment calculations are provided in the official NSE circular

## Important Dates

- **Record Date**: July 29, 2025
- **Ex-Date**: July 29, 2025
- **Last Date to Buy**: July 28, 2025
- **Rights Issue Opening**: August 6, 2025
- **Allotment Date**: August 21, 2025
- **Listing Date**: August 25, 2025

## Impact Assessment

The adjustment affects all existing Futures and Options positions in INOXWIND. While the contracts will be modified, the economic value of positions will be preserved through proportional adjustments to strike prices and lot sizes. This standard procedure ensures market integrity and protects investor interests during corporate actions. Traders should expect modifications to contract symbols to reflect the adjustments.
