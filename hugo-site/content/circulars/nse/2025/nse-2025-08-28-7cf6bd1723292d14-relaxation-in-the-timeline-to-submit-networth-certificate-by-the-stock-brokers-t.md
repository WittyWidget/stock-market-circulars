## Summary

NSE has relaxed the timeline for stock brokers to submit half-yearly networth certificates required to offer margin trading facility to clients. The new deadlines extend from the previous 30th April/31st October to 31st May/15th November, harmonizing with financial result declaration timelines under SEBI listing regulations.

## Key Points

- Timeline for networth certificate submission extended to provide operational relief
- New deadlines align with existing financial reporting requirements
- Certificate must confirm broker's networth as on 31st March and 30th September
- Changes effective immediately
- Based on market participant representations and ease of doing business initiative

## Regulatory Changes

- Modified para 4.5.3 of Chapter 1 of Master Circular for Stock Exchanges and Clearing Corporations (SECC)
- Previous deadline: Within 30 days (30th April for March, 31st October for September)
- New deadline: Within 60 days for March half-year (31st May) and 45 days for September half-year (15th November)
- Harmonized with Regulation 33 of SEBI LODR Regulations, 2015 timelines

## Compliance Requirements

- Stock brokers must submit half-yearly auditor certificate confirming networth
- Certificate dates: As on 31st March and 30th September each year
- Submission deadlines: Not later than 31st May and 15th November respectively
- Stock exchanges must amend relevant bye-laws and regulations
- Exchanges must notify members and publish on websites

## Important Dates

- **Effective Date**: August 28, 2025 (immediate effect)
- **Certificate Dates**: 31st March and 30th September annually
- **New Submission Deadlines**: 31st May and 15th November annually
- **SEBI Circular Reference**: SEBI/HO/MRD/MRD-PoD-2/P/CIR/2025/120 dated August 26, 2025

## Impact Assessment

- **Operational Relief**: Provides additional time for brokers to compile and submit required documentation
- **Compliance Harmonization**: Reduces regulatory burden by aligning with existing financial reporting cycles
- **Business Continuity**: Ensures smoother operations for brokers offering margin trading facilities
- **Market Efficiency**: Supports ease of doing business initiative while maintaining regulatory oversight
