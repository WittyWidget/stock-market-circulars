## Summary

SEBI has issued a final order under Section 12(3) of the SEBI Act, 1992 against Swaraj Shares and Securities Pvt. Ltd. (SEBI Registration No. INM000012980) following an inspection conducted from March 5-6, 2024. The order addresses multiple compliance violations identified during the inspection period from March 28, 2023 to January 31, 2024.

## Key Points

- SEBI conducted inspection of Swaraj Shares and Securities Pvt. Ltd., a registered Merchant Banker
- Inspection findings communicated on March 18, 2024 with company response submitted March 23, 2024
- Designated Authority appointed on April 19, 2024 to conduct enquiry proceedings
- Pre-enquiry show cause notice issued on May 9, 2024 with company reply dated September 22, 2024
- Personal hearing conducted on October 21, 2024 with additional submissions on October 25, 2024
- Final Enquiry Report submitted on November 11, 2024

## Regulatory Changes

No new regulatory changes introduced. This is an enforcement action addressing existing compliance requirements under SEBI (Merchant Banker) Regulations, 1992 and SEBI (Intermediaries) Regulations, 2008.

## Compliance Requirements

- Merchant bankers must exercise proper due diligence in takeover transactions
- Compliance with provisions pertaining to investor grievances is mandatory
- Accurate submission of periodic reports is required
- Full adherence to SEBI (Merchant Banker) Regulations, 1992 and applicable SEBI circulars

## Important Dates

- March 5-6, 2024: SEBI inspection conducted
- March 18, 2024: Inspection findings communicated to company
- March 23, 2024: Company response submitted

- September 22, 2024: Company reply to show cause notice

- November 11, 2024: Final Enquiry Report submitted
- December 6, 2024: Post-enquiry show cause notice issued

## Impact Assessment

This enforcement action demonstrates SEBI's continued focus on ensuring compliance by registered intermediaries. The violations identified include failure to exercise proper due diligence in takeover transactions, non-compliance with investor grievance provisions, and discrepancies in periodic reporting. While specific to one merchant banker, this serves as a reminder to all intermediaries about the importance of maintaining robust compliance frameworks and adherence to regulatory requirements.
