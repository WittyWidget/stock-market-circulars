## Summary

SEBI has issued a General Remittance Order dated 10.10.2025 under Recovery Certificate No. 5718 of 2022 against M/s. Metalman Industries Limited (PAN: AAACM9510G) in connection with non-redressal of investor grievances. This order is part of SEBI's enforcement and recovery proceedings against the company for its failure to address investor complaints and grievances in a timely manner.

## Key Points

- **Entity**: M/s. Metalman Industries Limited (PAN: AAACM9510G)
- **Order Type**: General Remittance Order
- **Recovery Certificate No.**: 5718 of 2022
- **Issue**: Non-redressal of investor grievances
- **Order Date**: 10th October 2025
- This is an enforcement action under SEBI's recovery proceedings framework
- The order relates to outstanding dues/amounts arising from unresolved investor grievances

## Regulatory Changes

No new regulatory changes are introduced. This is an enforcement action implementing existing SEBI regulations regarding:
- Investor protection and grievance redressal mechanisms
- Recovery proceedings under SEBI Act for non-compliance
- Corporate obligations towards investor grievance resolution

## Compliance Requirements

- **For Metalman Industries Limited**:
  - The company is subject to recovery proceedings under the Recovery Certificate No. 5718 of 2022
  - Must comply with the remittance order and fulfill obligations towards aggrieved investors
  - Required to settle outstanding investor grievances as per SEBI's directions
  - Non-compliance may lead to further enforcement actions including attachment of assets

- **For Investors**:
  - Aggrieved investors may claim their dues as per the recovery proceedings
  - Should submit necessary documentation to support their claims

## Important Dates

- **Order Date**: 10th October 2025
- **Recovery Certificate Issued**: 2022 (Recovery Certificate No. 5718 of 2022)
- **Compliance Deadline**: As specified in the detailed order (refer to PDF)

## Impact Assessment

**High Impact on Metalman Industries Limited:**
- The company faces serious enforcement action for failing to redress investor grievances
- Recovery proceedings can lead to attachment and sale of assets to recover dues
- Significant reputational damage and loss of investor confidence
- Potential restrictions on business operations until compliance is achieved
- May face additional penalties and restrictions from SEBI

**Impact on Investors:**
- Existing shareholders may see adverse impact on stock price and liquidity
- Aggrieved investors have a legal mechanism to recover their dues through the recovery certificate
- Potential investors should exercise extreme caution before investing in the company

**Market Impact:**
- This action reinforces SEBI's commitment to investor protection and strict enforcement against non-compliant entities
- Serves as a deterrent for other listed companies regarding timely grievance redressal
- Highlights the importance of robust investor grievance redressal mechanisms in listed companies
