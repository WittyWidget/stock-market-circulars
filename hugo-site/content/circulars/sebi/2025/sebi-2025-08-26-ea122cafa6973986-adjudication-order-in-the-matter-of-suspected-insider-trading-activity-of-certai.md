## Summary

SEBI issued an adjudication order against Mr. Arun Kumar Somani (Senior Finance Manager at IOCL), Mr. Ramesh Chand Gupta (father-in-law), and Ms. Tara Devi Gupta (mother-in-law) for suspected insider trading violations in Indian Oil Corporation Ltd. (IOCL) scrip. The case involves alleged communication of unpublished price sensitive information (UPSI) regarding Q2 2023-24 financial results and interim dividend declaration, followed by trading while in possession of such information.

## Key Points

- IOCL announced Q2 2023-24 financial results and 50% interim dividend (Rs. 5/- per equity share) on October 31, 2023 at 13:57:10 Hrs
- IOCL scrip price increased by 1.64% on announcement day and cumulatively by 17.90% till November 7, 2023
- Mr. Arun Kumar Somani was a designated employee involved in finalization and limited review of Q2 2023-24 results
- Alleged violations occurred during trading period from October 3, 2023 to November 15, 2023
- Mr. Somani allegedly communicated UPSI to his father-in-law Mr. Ramesh Chand Gupta
- Both in-laws allegedly traded in IOCL scrip while in possession of UPSI

## Regulatory Changes

No new regulatory changes introduced - enforcement action under existing PIT Regulations, 2015.

## Compliance Requirements

- Designated employees must strictly adhere to insider trading prohibition under PIT Regulations
- No communication of UPSI to connected persons or relatives
- Trading restrictions during possession of UPSI apply to all connected persons
- Enhanced monitoring and compliance framework for designated employees

## Important Dates

- October 31, 2023: IOCL announced Q2 2023-24 results and interim dividend
- October 3 - November 15, 2023: Alleged insider trading period
- November 7, 2023: Peak price appreciation (17.90% cumulative increase)
- August 26, 2025: Adjudication order issued

## Impact Assessment

This case demonstrates SEBI's continued vigilance against insider trading violations and serves as a deterrent for designated employees and their connected persons. The significant price movement (17.90% increase) following the results announcement highlights the market impact of UPSI. The case reinforces the importance of maintaining information barriers and compliance with PIT Regulations across corporate hierarchies and family networks.
